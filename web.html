Let's draw

Let's draw is a website produced in the interactivity class at IMAC. We had to make a mockup for an interactive project.

Two giant interactive screens full of light and colour, with music coming out of them, placed in two train stations in different countries, which allow passers-by to play Pictionary with their bodies. You can choose to play with people from the city you are going to or from another available city. Entertaining music comes out of this screen to attract people. The objective is to collect art supplies for an association that develops art in a disadvantaged context. How to play? It's very simple: several players from city A draw something with their body, the players in city B guess what it is. If they guess, the player from city A wins supplies for the art association.

I also made the website music for fun!

Outils : HTML, CSS, JavaScript, NodeJS, Express.js, Socket.io, P5.js, FLStudio

Blackjack

This project was carried out for our mathematics course at IMAC. We had to include in this project random variables and random variables and probability laws. This time, design was not something to really care about, I focused on probabilities.

Blackjack is a game governed by the hypergeometric law, since a sample of n-elements is drawn at random. We have an exhaustive draw of n-elements (i.e. n-draws without a discount). You can start the game with a 52-card deck or a 32-card deck. This blackjack follows the Las Vegas blackjack model. You will find in this report more explanations of the way we calculated probabilites and how we integrate random variables. (It is a french document because it was for a class at my University)
